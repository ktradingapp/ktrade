# KTrade — V13.12 (intraday safety rules)

From a 15-item rule review, this adds ONLY the three that are (a) genuine gaps,
(b) safety rather than edge — so low curve-fit risk, and (c) verifiable here. The
other 12 were deliberately NOT built (already handled, strategy choices to validate
on real data, or blocked on the news feed) — see the bottom.

All three are OFF by default and only relevant once you run intraday.

## #1 — Intraday end-of-day flatten (KTRADE_INTRADAY_EOD_FLATTEN=false)
Near the close (15:55–16:00 ET), on intraday intervals, open positions are
flattened so an intraday trade doesn't silently become an overnight swing exposed
to gaps. Best-effort (RTH orders only, so it acts if a cycle runs in that window).
Mirrors the existing earnings-ahead exit path.

## #13 — Pause new entries in the open/close minutes (KTRADE_AVOID_OPEN_CLOSE_MINUTES=false)
No NEW entries during the opening auction (first 5 min) or closing rush (last 10
min), when spreads are wide and prices erratic. Exits are unaffected.

## #5 — Liquidity floor (KTRADE_MIN_AVG_DOLLAR_VOL=0)
Downgrades a BUY to WATCH on a name whose average dollar volume (close*volume, 20
bars) is below the threshold — thin names have bad slippage. 0 disables (default);
suggested ~$2M/day if enabled.

## The 12 I did NOT build, and why
- **Already bounded / by-design:** per-position max loss (already bounded by ~$500
  risk-budget sizing + ATR stop), correlation-matrix sizing (sector caps exist;
  matrix is over-engineering), active regime sizing (regime is shadow BY DESIGN —
  gating on it before OOS evidence is the opposite of the discipline we built),
  relative-strength hard gate (RS is already a conviction component).
- **Strategy choices — validate on YOUR data first, don't add blind:** ATR trailing
  stops (contradicts the whipsaw tightening), market-wide trend filter (contradicts
  buy-the-pullback), partial profit-taking, scaling in/out, re-entry confirmation.
  Each changes the P&L profile; only your paper data can say if it helps.
- **Blocked on infrastructure:** news/sentiment hard filter needs a news feed wired
  into the agent (same constraint as the CatalystVerifier — a data integration on
  your side), and general gap-risk sizing needs historical gap data (earnings
  blackout already covers the biggest gap event).

## Honest framing (unchanged)
These three reduce specific intraday risks; none creates an edge. And they only
matter once intraday is ON — which is your LAST validation step, not your first.
Adding the other 12 now would be optimizing against imagined problems; the right
filter is "does my paper data show this?", and that data doesn't exist yet.
