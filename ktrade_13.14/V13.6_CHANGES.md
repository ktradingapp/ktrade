# KTrade — V13.6 (parameter-fragility sweep)

Directly serves the only question that matters next — "is the daily strategy's edge
real, or curve-fit?" KTrade's optimizer (ktrade_vectorbt.py) grid-searches and keeps
the SINGLE best Sharpe, which is exactly how you end up trading a lucky spike. This
adds the missing robustness check.

## New: agent/param_fragility.py (CLI + importable)
Sweeps a parameter grid and analyzes the WHOLE surface, not just the winner:
- **profitable_fraction** — share of parameter sets that are profitable at all;
- **plateau_ratio** — how much of the best result its immediate neighbours retain
  (high = a real plateau; low = a lonely spike);
- **cliff_fraction** — share of adjacent parameter pairs that flip
  profitable<->unprofitable (brittleness);
- **classification: ROBUST / MIXED / FRAGILE** (leans conservative — a real edge
  wants a high profitable%, plateau_ratio >= ~0.5, and a low cliff_fraction).

A FRAGILE verdict means the "edge" is curve-fit and won't survive live.

  python agent/param_fragility.py --prices NVDA.csv --strategy macd
        # CSV columns: date,close[,volume]   strategies: macd | ema | momentum | conviction

## Two layers, and what's verified
- **Engine** (`sweep` + `analyze_fragility`): pure, dependency-light, FULLY tested
  here. `test_v134_fragility.py` (**17/17**) proves it on synthetic surfaces with
  known answers — a smooth plateau reads ROBUST (plateau 0.87, 100% profitable); a
  lonely spike reads FRAGILE/curve-fit (plateau −0.1, 4% profitable) — plus no-trade
  handling and empty-result guards.
- **VectorBT adapter** (`make_vectorbt_backtest_fn`): wires the engine to KTrade's
  REAL gen_* strategies and `_run_portfolio` (with the existing one-bar shift that
  prevents look-ahead). Written against the actual signatures in ktrade_vectorbt.py,
  so it's correct — but it needs vectorbt + price data, so it runs on your VPS, not
  in CI. This is **verified-by-construction**, not verified-by-execution; I can't run
  a real backtest in this sandbox (no market-data network; yfinance unreliable from a
  datacenter IP).

## How this fits the priority list
This is the code half of step 1 ("validate the daily strategy"). Run it per ticker
and strategy on your historical data: if your approved params sit on a PLATEAU,
that's a (necessary, not sufficient) sign of a real edge; if they're a SPIKE, the
backtest Sharpe is fool's gold and no amount of execution hardening will save it.
