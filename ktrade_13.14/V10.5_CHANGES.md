## What v10.5 adds

### 1. Sector / correlation concentration cap (the important one)
Your RiskEngine capped per-position size (10%) and total position count (10),
but **nothing stopped it from putting all 10 positions into one correlated
basket** — e.g. NVDA + AMD + MU + AVGO + ARM + MRVL. On a day like June 22, 2026
(Nasdaq −1.3%, GOOGL −5%, AMZN −4.8%, MSFT −3%, while the Dow was *up*), those
names fall together and every stop hits together. That's a concentrated loss the
existing limits don't catch.

New in `RiskEngine.evaluate()` (step 10b), enforced **before** approval:
- **Per-sector position count** — default max **3** open positions per correlated
  basket (`MAX_POSITIONS_PER_SECTOR`).
- **Per-sector dollar exposure** — default max **30%** of equity in one basket
  (`MAX_SECTOR_EXPOSURE_PCT`), with a "nearing cap" warning at 80% of the limit.

Correlated baskets are defined in `_SECTOR_GROUPS` / `SECTOR_MAP` (AI_SEMI,
AI_CLOUD_SW, QUANTUM_SPACE, EV_AUTO, POWER_ENERGY, CRYPTO, FINANCIALS,
BIOTECH_HEALTH, INDEX_ETF, SAFE_HAVEN). Tickers not listed map to `OTHER` and are
exempt (sized individually). Tune the groups to match how you actually think
about correlation.

Both knobs are environment-overridable:
```
MAX_SECTOR_EXPOSURE_PCT=30
MAX_POSITIONS_PER_SECTOR=3
```
