# KTrade — V13.8 (validation pipeline / training harness)

Builds the one real gap between KTrade and the validation strategy: a single command
that chains the existing checks and emits one report. With a long runway and
confidence-before-capital as the goal, this is the central instrument for the
training phase, not a side tool.

## New: scripts/run_validation_pipeline.py + agent/strategy_validator.py
`python scripts/run_validation_pipeline.py` runs, in order:
1. **compile** — py_compile every module
2. **release_safety** — check_release_safety.py
3. **tests** — every suite, CI-style, isolated app-data
4. **backtest** — VectorBT backtest of one strategy (needs `--prices` + vectorbt)
5. **fragility** — param_fragility sweep -> ROBUST/MIXED/FRAGILE (FRAGILE => WARN)
6. **copilot** — copilot_analysis over a shadow ledger (needs paper-trading data)

It writes `reports/strategy_validation_report.md` with a verdict:
- **READY** (all executed checks passed), **REVIEW** (ran clean but flagged a WARN —
  e.g. a FRAGILE strategy, a human decision), **NOT_READY** (a check FAILED).

`agent/strategy_validator.py` holds the pure report/verdict logic, unit-tested.

  python scripts/run_validation_pipeline.py
  python scripts/run_validation_pipeline.py --prices NVDA.csv --strategy macd

## What's verified vs what runs on the VPS
- Stages 1-3 run for real ANYWHERE — verified in this sandbox: compile PASS,
  release_safety PASS, all suites PASS, READY verdict, report written.
- Stages 4-6 need inputs that only exist on the VPS (price data) or after paper
  trading (a shadow ledger), so they SKIP cleanly here and execute there. The report
  states plainly what ran vs skipped. Verified-by-execution for the skip handling;
  verified-by-construction for the data-stage wiring (reuses v13.6 param_fragility
  and v13.1 copilot_analysis, both already tested).

## Honest framing — what "training" and a READY verdict mean
KTrade is a rules + LLM-advisory system, not a neural net, so "training" here means
**accumulating validated evidence and tuning on it** — backtests, fragility checks,
and (most importantly) weeks of real paper-trading + shadow-ledger outcomes — not
gradient training. A READY verdict means the agent is **correct, safe, and not
obviously curve-fit**. It does NOT mean it's profitable. Profitability is shown only
by out-of-sample results and live paper outcomes over time. The honest goal of the
training phase is to FIND OUT whether the strategies have an edge and build justified
confidence either way — if the evidence says no edge, the confident move is to keep
iterating rather than risk capital. Confidence in the evidence, not faith in the bot.
